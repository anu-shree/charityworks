Rakshika: A pilot project on Anti Human Trafficking to make Rural Jharkhand Safe and Protected place for Women and Children

Context

Trafficking in women and children is one of the worst abuses of human rights. But it is very difficult to estimate the scale of the phenomenon as trafficking is closely related to child labour, bonded labour, child marriage, kidnapping and abduction, boys used in Goa ostensibly for fishing but also used in pedophilic activities, and sex work even though these phenomena can exist also independent of trafficking. This proposal is an attempt to analyze the nature, causes and modes of trafficking in Jharkhand state that has recently become a soft target in India for trafficking in persons and propose strategy for combating the heinous crime in the state. India has failed to comply with certain international standards to combat the crime. This proposal highlights the need to develop a multidimensional approach and focuses attention on structural factors of trafficking for recommending meaningful stratagems to counter the social evil.  

The crux of the problem is that human trafficking, being a highly secretive and clandestine trade, remains mostly under-reported and untraced in spite of the fact that several international initiatives have been taken to impel governments into action. Conversely, the increasing volume of human trafficking is also a reflection of the lack of political will on the part of the state as evident in the plethora of instances to allocate inadequate resources to combat the problem. The problem is therefore much deeper and wider than their meagre reportage to the police. There has been a global criticism about the Indian Government for not complying fully with the minimum standards for the elimination of trafficking. Not only is the issue of human trafficking in India a low priority for the law enforcement and justice delivery agencies, there is lack of synergy among various government departments like police, welfare, health,  labor, women and Child Development, etc. This has resulted in perfunctory and delayed investigations as well as dwindling rates of convictions. Of late, the government has suggested a new Anti-Human Trafficking law which has been passed in the lower house of Parliament but not in the upper house as on date. There is much need for debate on this proposed law as largely it contains segments that look like knee jerk reactions and hence tokenism.

Anti-trafficking law in Jharkhand   

Even though India has turned into a hotbed of human trafficking, the Constitution of the country has prohibited all sorts of trafficking under Article 23.  The  Suppression of Immoral  Traffic  Act  1956  (SITA), amended as the  Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act (ITPA) in 1978 and later in 1986, was in response to the ratification of the International Convention on Suppression of Immoral Traffic and Exploitation of Prostitution of Others in 1950. The amended law stressing prevention rather than suppression of human trafficking took into consideration the international conventions and protocols and provided severe penalties for different types of exploitative conditions. The ITPA toughened penalties for trafficking in children,  particularly  by  focusing  on  traffickers, pimps, landlords, and brothel  operators,  while  protecting  underage  girls  as victims.  The ITPA also provides protection and rehabilitation for the rescued girls, though in recent terms such protection is under scanner and question.  Apart from the ITPA, trafficking of women and children for sexual exploitation is covered under several other sections of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), namely, 366A (Procuring of Minor Girls Under 18 Years of Age), 366B (Importation of Girls Under 21 Years of Age From a Foreign Country), 367 (Kidnapping and Abduction for Slavery, Unnatural Lust, etc.), 372 (Selling of Minor Girls for Prostitutions), 373 (Buying of Minor Girls for Prostitutions). There are some other offences under IPC that have direct bearing on the problem of trafficking. Thus, for instance, trafficked women/girls very often are raped (an offence under section 376) before being sold to the brothel. Also sexual intercourse with a girl child under 16 years of age, even with her consent, constitutes the offence of a rape u/s 376 IPC. Similarly, special legislations like The Child Marriage Restraint Act 1929, Bonded Labour Abolition Act 1976, Child Labour Act 1986, Juvenile Justice Act 2000, POCSO and local legislations like Goa Children’s Act 2003 also attempt to prevent human trafficking.  

Strategies: 

The three strategies revolve around the three Ps viz., prevention, protection and prosecution.

  1. Prevention: Community organizations such as SHGs / VOs / CLFs and BLFs (where formed) can play a very pro-active role in their respective environs to effectively carry of activities that would prevent persons from unsafe migration thus reducing their vulnerability to trafficking. 
  2. Protection: Community organizations in consultation and support from line departments will ensure rehabilitation of survivors of trafficking in order for them to regain agency over their lives and lead a more purposeful life. Line departments would include WCD, CWC, and age specific rehabilitation through education or building skills for employment or integrate them into SHGs for economic support.
  3. Convergence with the concerned departments: Community cadres to liaise with District Legal Services Authority, AHTU of Police department to apprehend and prosecute identified traffickers and also assist police in identifying traffickers in destination points to register cases against them as well.

Key Activities:

  1. Mass Campaign and awareness generation about Human Trafficking, and other forms of violence against women and children would be organised
    1. Conducting Anti Human Trafficking Campaign at village level. Street Plays and Video Films will be shown in the VO and SHG meetings and School Assemblies at least once in 6 Months
    2. Dash board of the survivors will be maintained at the state level for regular tracking
    3. Registration of complain through tele-helpline will be initiated for the project areas. 
  2. Institutional Setup to be established for the prevention of trafficking:
    1. VLCP/BLCPCs are revived and members are properly trained
    2. Adolescent Girls and Boys will be formed into separate groups at the hamlet level. 
    3. Activity clubs based on the hobby of the adolescents would be formed.
    4. List of families vulnerable to trafficking would be identified through PRA and Government/Police Records 
    5. These vulnerable families would be encouraged to be part of SHGs, if not already and their access would be ensured to the provisions of NRLM
    6. BRP-SDs to do the regular follow up of these vulnerable families 
    7. Survivor wise plan is formed and tracked by the respective VO
    8. Help Desk is formed at the School level consisting of teachers and students 
    9. Registration of all the unorganised labourers by the PRI in convergence with Labour Department 

      3. Building of CBOs for addressing the trafficking issues

  1. Resource Organisations are empanelled through advertisements and hand picking
  2. Monthly review meeting and action plan is facilitated with the empanelled  ROs/RPs and
  3. Training and handholding calendars are formed for DRPs/BRPs, CBO leaders and SAC members.
  4. BRP-SDs are trained and placed @ 1 BRP for every 4 VOs
  5. SHGs are oriented and sensitized with the help of SAC members and Badlao Didi
  6. VO and SAC are oriented and sensitized with the help of BRP-SD 

 

       4. Proper tracking and networking mechanism is established at the village and higher level:

  1. Civil societies are identified and taken on board for seeking their help in all the three aspects of AHT viz Prevention, Protection and Prosecution.
  2. MTR, STRs and CTR[1] to be maintained at the VO level by the BRP-SD and Panchayat Animators
  3. The Registers are submitted to the Block Project Coordinators every Month for analysis
  4. The Analysis are Presented in Networking Meeting by the BPC
  5. Regular Monthly meeting with members of CWC and AHTU for the exchange of information 
  6. Monthly meeting of School Masters, SMC members, PRI Members, BRP-SD, VO SAC members, VO Office Bearers, PLV, Anganwadi Worker and Police Beat Officer would be conducted at the Panchayat level
  7. Every Child, inside or outside the school, will be tracked with the help of Child Tracking Register
  8. Police, DLSA, PRI and VO interface will be introduced on six monthly basis in each GP
  9. Block level Monthly Consultation of Stakeholders where Analysis of MTR, CTR and STR to be presented by the Special Cell and BPC / BPM
  10. Block Administration to seek ATR from the Gram Panchayat and Police on six monthly basis.
  11. District level Quarterly Consultation of Stakeholders where Analysis of MTR, CTR and STR to be presented by the DM-SD/DPM

         Establishing Migration Facilitation Cells at the panchayat level

  1. Migration Facilitating Cells will be formed at the Panchayat level for facilitating the members for safe migration. 
  2. BRP-SD, CLF and VO members and Panchayat Animators are to be trained on different labour act by the Department of Labour, Employment and Training, GoJ
  3. VO wise Online Registration is organised with the help of Data Entry Operators in a Camp Mode
  4. Villagers in general would be given orientation about the CLMS through street play and SHG/VO level discussions
  5. VO wise Online Registration is organised with the help of Data Entry Operators in a Camp Mode
  6. Labour Contractors would also be identified through migrant labourers and would be oriented and motivated for registration for seeking license
  7. With the help of VO and CLF the domestic labourers and other unorganised workers are identified at each village and are oriented on safe migration
  8. Life Skill and Skill Upgradation trainings are imparted through DDU-GKY

 

         5. Rights and entitlements of women & children are ensured

  1. Based on the individual profile of the vulnerable families their eligibility against different schemes would be mapped using GEET. 
  2. Action plan regarding the entitlements would be presented in the public hearing at block and district level.                                                                
  3. ATRs regarding entitlements would be presented at Block and district level public hearing
  4. workshop with children and school teachers will be conducted on Child Rights, Human Trafficking, child marriage
  5. Help Desk would be formed in every school for taking track of the children on the verge of getting dropped out
  6. Life skill training for adolescents out of school and in schools
  7. Nurturing Life skills and consciousness about their rights amongst Children
  8. Children clubs are formed for School Going Children as well as for Children outside the school (drop out or never been to school)
  9. Children are encouraged and engaged by the CLF for their social and legal orientation 
  10. Regular informal tuitions are conducted with the help of School Masters, Retd Govt Officials and other volunteers

 

         6. Convergence with AHTU/DLSA and Police for effective legal interventions

  1. Cases are reported to Special Cell for immediate action
  2. Social and legal Professionals are placed in all the PS
  3. Socio Legal Framework for dealing with trafficking is framed
  4. Orientation of Partner Organisations (legal, Medical, Civil Societies, Police, etc.) on the issues
  5. Road map for cases reaching to Special Cell is prepared and executed
  6. Professionals of the cell to personally visit the  victims as per the road map
  7. Vulnerable family wise service plan is prepared
  8. Network of Civil Societies is identified and mapped for ensuring required services to the survivors
  9. Police Community interfaces to be organised in every Panchayat twice in a year
  10. Legal counselling of all the survivors would be conducted with the help of DLSA and legal experts
  11. Legal Case documents are prepared with the help of Para Legal Workers/Volunteers based on the legal counselling
  12. Legal action plan of the survivors is made and executed as per the counselling 

7. Stakeholder consultations are organised for inter departmental coordination and policy advocacy

  1. Block level stakeholder's consultation are organised every Month wherein Panchayat level consultations are presented
  2. District consultation meeting would be held in every quarter where the challenges emerged from the block level consultations are presented
  3. State level consultation to be held in every six months where in Learning from the district level consultations and support they require from the state would be presented
  4. National Level consultations would be held with other SRLMs, National level Civil Societies and Government of India for advocating the policy needs at the national and state levels
  5. Inter SRLM coordination is held for providing destination level services. 
  6. Joint Review Committee consisting of representatives of representatives from all the relevant department and civil societies is formed
  7. The review committee to forward the suggestions to the cabinet through the concerned department for consideration 

[1] Migration Tracking Register (MTR), Survivor Tracking Register (STR) and Child Tracking Register (CTR)
