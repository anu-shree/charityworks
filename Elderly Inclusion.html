Elderly Inclusion Process in Jharkhand under DAY-NRLM

 

    Demography:

Senior Citizens i.e. people in the age group of 60 years and above forms 6.7% of the entire population of Jharkhand as per Census-2011. The same is 8% at the national level. In absolute terms the elderly population in Jharkhand is about 22 Lakhs out of which 16.53 Lakhs elderly people belong to the rural areas of Jharkhand.  Unlike most of the state, in Jharkhand, elderly male population is little higher than the elderly female population. In Rural Jharkhand Elderly female population is about 8.1 lakh as per the census 2011. As per Ministry of Statistics and Programme Implementation (MoSPI)   of the Government of India survey - 2016, the literacy rate of elderly population is only 27.2% in Rural Jharkhand and the ratio of literate male population to that of female population is about 3:1. As per the Office of Registrar General, India, the Old Age Dependency Ratio in Rural Jharkhand in 2011 was 13.4% i.e. for every 100 person in the productive age there are more than 13 elderly person to be taken care of. Which means burden on the working population is high in Jharkhand. 

With the poor literacy rate, high old age dependent ratio, poor health facilities, the increasing vulnerability of the elderly population of Rural Jharkhand cannot be denied.

 

    Intervention in Jharkhand under the DAY-NRLM: 

      There has been two kinds of approach in Jharkhand under DAY-NRLM for the elderly inclusion. 

  1. Special drive in partnership with HelpAge India.
  2. Elderly group formation as part of normal SM&IB drive

  1. Special drive in partnership with HelpAge India:

  • JSLPS entered into partnership with HelpAge India in July 2015. 
  • The entire process initiated with senior staff of JSLPS going to Tamilnadu to the field exposure on elderly inclusion to HPI area. After the MoU was signed, it was planned to give similar exposure to the ground team of JSLPS along with the community representatives for their greater understanding on the issue and motivating them for the larger purpose of elderly inclusion.
  • The partnership with HPI was for 6 Clusters of 3 Blocks of 3 Districts of the state.
  • The HAI placed 6 Professional Resource Persons (PRPs) in each of the identified clusters.
  • The PRPs were dedicated for the elderly group formation and nurturing. 
  • The process was to identify the active women from the targeted village of the cluster and send them to Bihar, in the field area of HPI for 10 days for exposure cum training on elderly inclusion. 
  • While returning, the active women returned with elder resource person (ERP) from Bihar. The external Elderly Resource Person and the active woman were responsible for mobilising elderly population for the group formation in their targeted village.
  • After the group was formed, the group was to be linked to the concerned Village Organisations (VO). In order to smoothen the process VO leaders were also taken for similar exposure to Bihar and training. The helped JSLPS in motivating the VOs to work for the cause of the elderly inclusion. 
  • So far 19 Community Coordinators (CCs) and Cluster level Coordinators (CLCs), 137 Internal CRPs (largely active women) and 127 VO leaders were given exposure and training by HPI from the 6 clusters. 
  • Altogether 7097 elderly people from the three clusters were formed into 535 Elderly groups with the help of HPI.
  • Apart from the group formation a detailed survey was conducted to know their entitlement, health, education and livelihood profile. 513 member of the groups have started receiving their old age pension with the effort of PRPs. Application for another 93 member are pending with the department. 
  • Efforts were made for their account opening. This was a big challenge as many groups didn’t have any literate person in their group and the bank wouldn’t accept their application for account opening in the absence of any literate person in the group.  While the issue was brought to the notice of SLBC through Financial Inclusion team of JSLPS, a parallel drive was also initiated to make people literate in Barkela Cluster of West Singhbhum. So far 17 people were taught to do signature in the cluster. Bank A/C of 345 Elderly groups are opened eventually out of 535 groups. 

 

  1. Elderly group formation as part of normal SM&IB drive

  • As per the MIS of JSLPS, 2033 Elderly groups have been formed so far of 21,837 members in 1004 Villages of 540 Gram Panchayats of 122 Blocks of 23 Districts.
  • Out of the 2033 Elderly groups 2014 groups have been formed after 2012 i.e. under the DAY-NRLM period. 
  • The range of the age of the group id from 1 month to 198 months. 
  • However, 838 of these 2033 groups have not met since last more than 6 months and there is no record regarding the date of their last meeting of 942 groups. 
  • Out of these 2033 groups 247 have received OTMCs, 1083 have received RF and 112 have received CIF. 
  • Bank linkage is done for 118 Groups. However, only 20 groups have the services of book keepers.

 

Challenges and way forward:

  1. A long term strategy for the comprehensive development of elderly population and reduction of their vulnerabilities needs to be tabled.  The plan should focus on the rights and entitlements, health, food and nutrition security and the self-esteem of the elderly population. 
  2. Training all the groups on group activities and linking them with VO. 
  3. To ensure the services of book keepers for the group.
  4. To develop a calendar of activities for the elderly group for their regular and meaningful engagement.
  5. The 82 BRPs groomed for the elderly inclusion needs to be engaged for the training of elderly groups.
  6. The NRLM provisions are to be channelized to the elderly groups in priorities. 
  7. Policy advocacy regarding elderly care to safe guard their rights and interests to be vouched for.
