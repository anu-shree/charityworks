Garima: The project to make Jharkhand free from Witch Hunting and branding practices


CONTEXT

The rural area of Jharkhand suffers from several types of vulnerabilities. Branding of women as ‘witch’ is one of the biggest vulnerabilities. Witch hunting is a splotch on the face of Jharkhand. The evil practice is mostly found in the districts of West Singhbhum, Latehar, Ranchi, Khunti, Gumla, Lohardaga, Saraikela-Kharsawan, Bokaro, East Singhbhum and Simdega. 

Though the Malpractice is prevalent in several other states (12 as per NCRB), the undivided Bihar was the first state to form an Act against it in 1999. Jharkhand adopted the same in 2001. The law is called ‘Prevention of Witch (Daain) Practices Act, 2001’.

As per National Crime Record Bureau (NCRB) of India total recorded murders in the name of witch Hunting in India is 2022 out of which In Jharkhand alone the figure is 523. However, these are only reported figures, there cannot be any denial of the cases which might have gone unreported. As per civil societies there has been over 1800 murders in Jharkhand in past 18 years of its existence! That means ‘every third day a woman is lynched or murdered in the name of Witch Hunting’!

On an average every villages in Jharkhand would have 03 women who would be branded as witch and who are leaving there life in threat and isolation. Branding is the first stage of hunting. After having branded these women and their family members along with their children would live a life of threat and humiliation. Even death of a goat or a chicken can convince the villagers that these women have been practicing witch craft and are behind the death of that goat or that chicken. This slightest of hint has the capability to trigger the mob to rape them, torcher them, and finally lynch them in public. In Jharkhand where there are more than 32000 villages, we can say that there would be more than 1 Lakh women who have been branded as witch and are living their life in humiliation, isolation and under threat of life.

One just need to be a woman to be branded as witch. Therefore while currently there would be just 1 lakh women who are branded as witch but in fact every woman is potential victim of this malpractice. It just takes some Ojha or some family member or some agitated neighbour to brand anyone as witch. Once the woman is branded it doesn’t take much time to make others believe this too. Therefore it is not just about the 1 lakh women but about every single woman of the state and their rights. 

 In this context, it is important to address the issue with the help of Community Based Organisation (CBOs) in a way that no public lynching can take place for these women who have been branded as ‘Witch’ and secondly, these women can lead a dignified life henceforth having access to all the basic rights and entitlements that they are eligible for. Additionally, a sincere effort needs to put in to eliminate the whole possibility of even anyone being hinted as ‘Witch’. 

Hence the proposal on the project of making the state free from witch hunting and branding practices called ‘Garima’ is being put up to MoRD to be funded from Innovation fund under DAY-NRLM.

Rationale: JSLPS is working for Witch Hunting Prevention since May 2017. We formed theatre groups by training the interested SHG members on theatres and legal aspects of Witch Hunting, These theatre groups were then sent to the identified villages for 2 days 2 nights for performing street play in every corner and important places of the village as decided by the concerned VO of the village.  So far about 500 villages have been covered from 35 CLFs of 18 Blocks of 6 districts. Till now 932 survivors of the Witch Hunting have been identified through the campaign. Public hearings were conducted at the block levels for ensuring entitlements and due rights to the survivors. In 2018 and 2019 we had conducted impact analysis of the campaign in the life of each of these survivors with the help of interns from the universities. Through the impact analysis was done for more than 500 survivors, there were mixed responses. After discussing with the survivors, their family members, fellow SHG members, villagers, PRI members and other stake holders like Police, Women and Child Development dept,  District Legal Service Authority, education dept, Health Dept, DLSA etc. overall it was concluded that 

  • a strong follow up is required for the survivors
  • Each survivor needs to be nurtured and groomed through a well-structured plan for their counselling and livelihood through multiple round of trainings and Capacity Building. 
  • The campaign should be an annual activities to re-establish the importance of the issue. 
  • All the stakeholders needs to be taken on board for creating large support base for the survivors. 
  • More number of villages needs to be covered under the campaign. 
  • A helpline to be established so that the survivors won’t have to wait for the campaign to begin. The helpline should be able to provide immediate necessary support and protection to the survivors.
  • A large base of survivors shall emerge who could head on with the issue of Witch Hunting with activated sensitivity. 
  • A protection teams to be formed at the village level for regular social counselling of the community. The protection team shall keep a watch on the branding practices. They would immediately come forward for the help of any survivor in case of any threat like situation. 
  • Counselling of the survivors and its family. 

However, all these activities would require dedicated budget and project functionaries. Hence the proposal is created under innovation fund. 

Expected Outcomes of the Project:

 

  1. 5000 survivors are identified from 2068 villages during 3 years of project and are covered and protected under the CBOs created under NRLM.
  2. By the end of 2nd year each of the 342 Gram Panchayat and 25 Project Blocks will be declared as 'Witch Hunt Free' by the panchayat and district administration
  3. Income of the all the identified Survivors to be enhanced by 50% initiating at least two livelihood activities for each survivors.
  4. Entitlements of about 80% of survivors are ensured
  5. at least 50% of 'Violence Against Women & Children' cases being reported would be legally solved within 60 Days of reporting
  6. At least 10000 youths are leading campaign against Witch Hunting to bring transformational change in the society
  7. State Policy For Witch Hunting Prevention is framed based on the learning of the project


Key Strategies and Activities of the Project:
  1. Multiple approach to be adopted for the identification of victims
    1. CLF/BLF will campaign in the villages where they have their VOs for last more than 2 years with the help of their own team for the campaign who would have been trained in theatre and legal aspects of the Witch Hunting.
    2. Survivor’s Tracking Register (STR) is to be maintained at the VO level. The confidentiality of the register would be maintained by the VO.
    3. A call centre based Help line will be initiated for the survivors with the help of some Technical Support Agency. The Call centre will be loaded with the system and protocol regarding movement and registration of complaints received. 
    4. As per the requirement the complaint may be directed to VO or CLF or even higher order institution to investigate the complaints. 
    5. List of single/separated/women, rape victims, mentally challenged women, family members of survivors would be collected from the VO and Government/Police Records. They may be treated as potential victim.
    6. The list of Ojha and other people who have the past records of branding other people as Witch would also be maintained and would be trained on the legalities of the activities.
  2. Badlao Manch to be formed at every village:
    1. SAC, Badlao Didi, VO leaders, ANM, Anganwadi worker, School headmaster/ teacher (lady) and elected PRI representative (preferably woman) to be organised and trained as Badlao Manch.
    2. The manch will give immediate protection to the victim in case of emergency.
    3. The Badlao Manch would also prepare the list of women who have been branded as witch and will send their names to VO or CLF for the follow up actions
    4. The committee would also identify the people behind such branding and would send their names too to the VO/CLF for needful action.
    5. The badlao manch shall ensure that institutional norms formed by the CLF against Witch hunting is being followed in every SHG.
    6. The Badlao Manch shall advocate for such norms to be introduced in Gram Sabha and Gram Panchayat too
  3. Formation of Special Cell at the Police Station with the help of TSA:
    1. 2 professionals having the background of law and Social Work respectively would form a cell at the local select Police Station. The cell will work in coordination with Block Resource Person –Social Development (BRP-SD) and CBOs. 
    2. The professions will encourage people to report their cases to the police stations. 
    3. The cell will also sensitize police and with their help will organise Police Community interface for awareness building of villagers and survivors.
  4. Multiple doses of trainings and awareness generation activities:
    1. With the help of Badlao Didi (Gender Point persons), Office Bearers of SHGs and VOs, Active Woman and BRP-SD regular training and orientation programme would be organised at the SHG and village level
    2. The special cell with the help of Police and District Legal Service Authority (DLSA) will organise the interface between PRI, Police, DLSA, CBOs and community on the legal aspect of the issue.
    3. The CLF will organise the village camp of the Campaigning team once in every six months for the awareness generation.
    4. TSAs will organise such programme in school and village with the help of youth organisations and Badlao Manch.
    5. Legal Literacy and action Club will be established in select schools of every village and one collage of each block and district. The objective of the club is orient the children about the legal aspects of the issue and the science behind the activities of Ojha. 
  5. Cascading approach  for Capacity Building 
    1. One Block Resource Persons-Social Development (BRP-SD) will be placed to look after 4 VOs.
    2. Social Action Sub-Committee of Village Organisations (VOs) will be strengthened and more members would be added to it. 
    3. One Badlao Didi to be identified and trained from every SHG. They would, in turn, train all their fellow members of the SHG
    4. Modules, training calendar and IEC are developed to train BRP-SD, VO Members, SAC members and SHG Members.
    5. Video shows, posters, handbills, wall painting, flip books having illustrations and photographs will be developed and used for village level trainings.
  6. Public Hearings at Block, District and state levels for setting administrative accountability on case to case basis. 
    1. Block level public hearing would be organised on quarterly basis to hear about the victims as well as about the persons involved in such branding
    2. Block Administration to seek ATR from the Gram Panchayat and Police on six monthly basis.
    3. District level public hearing would be organised on six monthly basis 
  7. District administration to design norms for declaring Village/GP and Block as ‘Witch Hunt Free’ 
    1. Norms will be introduced against branding someone as Witch in every SHGs
    2. Oath against Witch Hunting will be introduced in SHGs, VOs and CLFs.
    3. Oath Against Witch Hunting will also be taken in all the Schools of the Project area
    4. Annual Campaign against Witch Hunting will be organised at each village
    5. Street Plays and Video Films will be shown in the VO and SHG meetings and School Assemblies at least once in 6 Months
    6. Special Gram Sabha is conducted to assess the status of action plan against each survivors of Witch Hunting
    7. The Special Gram Sabha would be used as forum to be used for declaring the village as Witch Hunt Free Village by the Gram Pradhan
    8. Oath against Witch Hunting will be introduced in project Gram Sabha and GP Executive Meeting.
    9. Strict Norms against 'witch branding' will be introduced by the Gram panchayat and traditional panchayat.
    10. SHG and VO will forward the names of persons involved in branding someone as witch to Gram Sabha/Panchayat for action
    11. Police, DLSA, PRI and VO interface will be introduced on six monthly basis in each GP
    12. All the survivors would be formally honoured and accepted by the Gram Sabha/ Panchayat.
    13. District Administration to give awards to the VO/ Panchayat declared as 'Witch Hunt Free'
  8. Survivor wise action plan to be developed after counselling and Livelihood Plan Drive
    1. Block Level Counselling of Survivors to be organised for making their career path
    2. As per the choice of survivors they would be trained as community cadre (Pashu Sakhi, Bank Sakhi, Kisan Mitra, CRP, Book Keeper, Campaign Team Members, etc.)
    3. Survivors to be ranked on the basis of the criticality of their case.
    4. A Sum of Rs. 20000 per survivor would be ear marked for the critical survivors at the CLF level from CIF
    5. Livelihood Plans of each survivors to be facilitated by the BRPs and verified by the VO and CLF
    6. VRF Fund to be given to the critical survivors in priority on 0% interest
    7. Special drive for bank linkage and preparing necessary documents to be initiated with the help of BC Sakhi
    8. Names of the interested persons are collected for the skill based training as per their areas of interests 
    9. Participants are supported for their start-ups or job placements
  9. Partnership with likeminded organisations
    1. Joint Partnership of JSLPS with, TSA, Civil Societies and CBOs
    2. Tie up with medical institutes and protection homes for the referral services
    3. Socio Legal Framework for dealing with VAW will be formed
    4. Network of Civil Societies is identified and mapped for ensuring required services to the survivors
    5. Orientation of Partner Organisations (legal, Medical, Civil Societies, Police, etc.) on the issues
    6. Road map for cases reaching to Special Cell is prepared and executed
  10. Sustainability in approach:
    1. The project will prepare the institutions against all kinds of Violence Against Women by:
      1. Building their understanding on various laws and legal procedures to access their rights.
      2. Building the capacities of SHG members and giving them a vision to handle the social issues.
      3. This will prepare the youth and children to take stand against the social evils.
      4. A strong rapport of CBOs with Police, PRI and Administration will make the women in general and survivors in particular more empowered in its true sense.
    2. The Badlao Manch at every village and organised youth volunteers under every CLF will give sense of confidence to survivors and would enable CBOs to handle the situations in more effective manner.
    3. Involving Survivors of Witch Hunting as well as survivors of other kinds of violence in the campaign as CRP would boost their socio-economic conditions.
